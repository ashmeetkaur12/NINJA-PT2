OVERVIEW OF THE PROBLEM

Neonatal jaundice affects over 60% of newborns worldwide and occurs due to the accumulation of bilirubin in the bloodstream. Timely diagnosis is crucial, as untreated jaundice can lead to conditions like kernicterus or long-term neurological damage.

Existing diagnostic methods require invasive blood sampling, often involving multiple pricks, causing discomfort to the infant and requiring clinical infrastructure that may not always be available in low-resource settings.

AIM OF THE PROJECT

The NINJA project is committed to create an AI-driven, non-invasive, and accessible system to estimate bilirubin levels through simple skin images of neonates. This approach is intended to reduce the need for invasive testing and expand early screening capabilities to even the most remote healthcare centers.

AI-BASED APPROACH

NINJA applies advanced deep learning techniques, particularly convolutional neural networks like ResNet, trained on skin images to estimate Total Serum Bilirubin (TSB) levels.

The model processes image features such as skin tone, lighting variations, and texture patterns to predict bilirubin levels accurately, offering a real-time and pain-free diagnostic tool.

ETHICAL GUIDELINES

1. All image data is collected after obtaining informed consent from parents or guardians.

2. Faces and any identifiable features are anonymized before training.

3. Data is securely stored in the cloud using Base64 encoding.

4. The project adheres to the biomedical ethical standards laid out by ICMR and institutional review boards.
