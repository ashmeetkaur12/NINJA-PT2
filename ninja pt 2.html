NON INVASIVE NEONATE JAUNDICE ESTIMATION

Non-invasive neonate jaundice estimation is a method used to assess jaundice in newborns without the need for blood draws. Jaundice, caused by elevated bilirubin levels, is common in newborns and can lead to serious complications if left untreated. Traditionally, bilirubin levels were measured through blood samples, but non-invasive techniques like transcutaneous bilirubinometry now offer a painless alternative. This method reduces discomfort, minimizes infection risk, and allows for frequent monitoring, especially in preterm or at-risk infants.
Main aim of project is to predict bilirubin level of infant which in turn will predict jaundice. of their body parts (Face , Sternum and Full Body) are clicked with the help of phone, which in turn will predict bilirubin level.These are captured at various locations using the same device to ensure consistency in the data collected. By analyzing these , the system aims to accurately predict the bilirubin levels, providing a non-invasive, efficient, and reliable method for jaundice estimation in newborns. This approach not only enhances the comfort and safety of the infants but also offers a practical solution for parents and healthcare providers to monitor jaundice more effectively.
